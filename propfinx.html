Why PropFinX comes first

SkyNest needs a financing layer before it can become a service-ready, income-producing asset.

Capital unlocks the building conversion

The sponsor needs funding for renovation, furnishing, smart-device installation, and short-stay setup. PropFinX is the layer that introduces the capital needed to move the project from concept into execution.

Compliance becomes part of the workflow

Instead of treating regulation as a separate manual process, PropFinX builds KYC, AML, investor eligibility, and transfer rules into the financing path itself.

Digital structure improves visibility

Once the financing rights are tokenized, issuance events, ownership records, and governance conditions remain far more legible across the lifecycle of the project.

SkyNest Capital Chapter

The sponsor submits one project, and PropFinX structures the full capital entry path.

In the case narrative, SkyNest begins when the sponsor submits a property conversion project and financing request to EstateX. PropFinX then carries the case from review to investor participation to issuance.

Without this financing step, SkyNest cannot move forward into furnishing, service setup, or rental operation.

01

Sponsor Submission

The project enters the system as a financing request.

The sponsor submits the building conversion project, funding objectives, and supporting materials. This creates the initial digital record of the opportunity and defines the capital needed to prepare SkyNest for later operational use.

02

Review and Admission

Due diligence and investor eligibility shape who can participate.

After review, investors complete wallet registration, KYC, AML, and whitelist approval before entering the offering. PropFinX ensures that access is controlled before any tokenized participation begins.

03

Issuance and Traceability

Smart contracts formalize the financing structure.

Approved financing rights are represented through digital tokens. Ownership is recorded, transfer restrictions are enforced, and the capital layer becomes transparent enough to support the rest of the EstateX flow.
