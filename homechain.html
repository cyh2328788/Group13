Why HomeChain is essential

Capital only creates value when property services are fulfilled at operational quality.

Funding must become physical delivery

The SkyNest case makes this very clear. After funding, the sponsor still needs furnishing, smart devices, cleaning support, and move-in readiness before any apartment can be listed or monetized.

Service work needs shared rules

HomeChain Store gives the project a governed marketplace with standardized service bundles, fulfillment logic, and payment paths so preparation is not left to fragmented offline coordination.

The service layer remains active after launch

HomeChain does not disappear once setup ends. In the case, StayLedger can continue calling cleaning or maintenance services through the same layer during ongoing operations.

SkyNest Use Case: Service & Fulfillment Layer

HomeChain is where the building starts becoming a finished hospitality product.

In the illustrative SkyNest use case, once financing is completed, the sponsor uses HomeChain Store to purchase the services and goods needed to prepare SkyNest for rental operations. This is the bridge between funded intention and physical readiness.

HomeChain Store connects the capital raised in PropFinX to actual service fulfillment in the physical world.

01

Order Formation

The sponsor translates operational needs into structured service orders.

Furniture packages, smart locks, cleaning, move-in kits, and staging support are selected through the marketplace. The service layer begins by making these practical needs visible and purchasable inside one system.

02

Fulfillment Logic

Payment and delivery are tied together by digital redemption records.

After payment, the platform can create a redemption record, service token, or coupon-style token linked to the purchased service, so the transaction does not end at checkout but continues into execution.

03

Settlement

Once work is complete, the economic flow is distributed automatically.

Smart contracts can release payouts to the merchant, installer, logistics provider, cleaner, and platform operator. The result is a property that is not only funded, but actually ready to operate.

Operating Sequence

How HomeChain Store executes fulfillment within the EstateX solution.

Step 1

Vendor Onboarding

Merchants and service providers join the marketplace with defined categories and service scope.

Step 2

Bundle Listing

Standardized furniture, staging, and service packages are listed with pricing and delivery terms.

Step 3

Purchase Flow

The sponsor or operator selects what SkyNest needs and pays with supported digital methods.

Step 4

Redemption Record

A digital record links the purchase to the exact service or goods expected in fulfillment.

Step 5

Completion Check

Service confirmation ensures that delivery is tied to actual execution and acceptance.

Step 6

Automated Payout

Settlement can be distributed across all participating parties when the work is confirmed.

Governance

Preparation becomes scalable when vendors, proof, and payouts follow the same rules.

Vendor Governance

Provider onboarding, service categorization, and quality expectations help keep execution consistent.

Fulfillment Governance

Completion confirmation and redemption logic reduce the gap between payment and physical delivery.

Settlement Governance

Smart-contract payout logic makes multi-party preparation work easier to reconcile and audit.
